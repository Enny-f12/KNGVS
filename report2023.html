2022 Report


Game-Changing Edition KNGVS 5.0 2022 We laid the ground work to expand to 5 summer vacation schools in Ibadan. This game-changing new initiative in the 2022 edition enabled us to reach a larger number of young children, and empower more families, elementary school teachers, and volunteers in our community. If we 'catch them young' by helping them acquire healthy dietary habits and skills in childhood, we will have a generation prepared to lead a life free of unprecedented health problems in the future.

KNGVS partnered with five schools in Ibadan, at which we organised hands-on educational Nutri-garden™ activities, to enrich and make their vacation schools worthwhile experiences for their pupils! From 1 August to 6 September , 2022, we engaged the kids in the Nutri-garden™-2-Fork adventure - hands-on gardening, nutriton education and healthy cooking/ food demonstration, as part of their vacation school activities.

KNGVS Project Report

KNGVS volunteers make us thrive. In the 2022 edition, we launched an expanded training for twenty-three young and passionate volunteers (the KNG Corps) to help implement Nutri garden™-2-fork adventure in the long vacation activities of the five partner schools. Our efforts resulted in a vibrant holiday experience for the schools and the children.

KNG Corps dedicated their time and energy throughout August and the first week of September, to set up and nurture six nutri gardens to grow local veggies. They helped the children to link the growing of veggies (...and foods in general) to nutrition concepts that promote their well-being and health.

The icing on the cake! healthy cooking/ food demonstration sessions where the KNG Corps assisted the kids to use veggies harvested from the nutri-garden to prepare simple healthy meals and snacks that they loved!!!. This aspect of KNGVS helps drive the "From nutri-garden-to-fork!" concept home.

A newsletter which summarised the week's learning and recipe, were given to the kids to take home for family activities at the end of each week.

Twenty nutri-garden & interactive nutri-education lessons, ten healthy cooking/ food demonstrations sessions, six open garden days, ten guest lectures, one excursion, one garden-2-fork exhibition

More Progress

It was even more fun when researchers from different institutions visited, to take the children through diverse aspects of learning about the garden environment. Dr Olajumoke Alabi, an entomologist from the Department of Crop Protection and Environmental Biology, UI, was at hand to talk to the children about GARDEN FRIENDS AND FOES, referring to good and bad insects. She also taught the kids songs and how to make ladybirds and bugs!!! When Mrs Nkechi Isienyi and her research team visited from the Department of Tree and Soil Nutrition, Forestry Research Institute of Nigeria (FRIN), it was time to take the children through the importance and care of soil, as well as how to make composts for achieving bountiful harvests.


"I am excited that my daughter is learning about gardening and relationship with the environment" -Parent

We also had exciting new content additions like the open garden day. Parents and the general public were invited to participate and learn gardening and nutrition from the children. Our guests also got their hands dirty harvesting some fresh veggies from the kids' Nutri-garden™. It was fun all the way!

Garden-2-Fork Exhibition

And it's a wrap!!!! KNGVS 5.0 was concluded in grand style with an exhibition and funfair. The event featured cook-with-me contest (parent and child pair) between partner schools, creative performances by the children, exhibition and sale of fresh veggies and healthy snacks from the kids nutri-garden. Exhibitors included Friesland Campina, nutri-preneurs, and agripreneurs. One of our major sponsors, Golden Penny Foods was also on ground to add colour to the day for the children.
